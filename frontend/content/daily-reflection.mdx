Daily reflection is a small habit with compounding value. Ten quiet minutes at the end of the day can expose patterns that are invisible while moving through the day.

## What Happened

The first prompt is factual: what happened today? This keeps the practice grounded before interpretation begins.

## What Mattered

The second prompt asks what actually mattered. Often, the important moment was smaller than the loudest moment.

## What Changes

The final prompt turns reflection into action: what should be repeated, adjusted, or released tomorrow?
