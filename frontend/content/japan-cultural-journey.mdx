Three weeks in Japan revealed how modern speed and old rituals can exist side by side without feeling contradictory.

## Tokyo

Tokyo felt precise, layered, and constantly moving. The city rewards attention: quiet alleys, tiny restaurants, and thoughtful systems are everywhere.

## Kyoto

Kyoto slowed the trip down. Temples, gardens, and older streets made it easier to notice the role of repetition and restraint in daily life.

## Osaka

Osaka brought warmth and directness. Food, conversation, and street energy made the final part of the journey feel grounded.
